📚 Key Lessons

1

Always Verify URLs

Check the URL bar carefully. Phishers use domains that LOOK legitimate but aren't.

2

Never Click Urgency Links

Phishing emails create artificial urgency. If urgent, go directly to the official website.

3

Companies Don't Ask for Passwords

Legitimate organizations never ask for passwords via email or web forms.

4

Check for Personalization

Real emails address you by name. Generic greetings are a red flag.

5

Look for HTTPS

Real secure sites have a padlock icon and HTTPS. Always verify!

6

Trust Your Instincts

If something feels off, it probably is. Take time to verify before acting.

🛡️ How to Protect Yourself

  • Bookmark Official Sites: Bookmark the official websites of organizations you use frequently. Go through bookmarks instead of clicking links.
  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication: Enable MFA on important accounts. Even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account.
  • Use a Password Manager: Password managers only fill passwords on legitimate sites, not phishing sites.
  • Enable Email Filters: Use email spam filters and train them to recognize phishing.
  • Hover Over Links: Before clicking, hover over links to see where they actually go.
  • Report Suspicious Emails: Report phishing emails to your organization's IT security team.
  • Keep Software Updated: Update your browser and operating system regularly for security patches.
