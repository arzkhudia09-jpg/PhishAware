🛡️ Security Best Practices

Essential tips to protect yourself from phishing and cyber threats

Use this page as your quick security playbook. Practical, bite-sized advice helps you stay alert and avoid phishing traps without getting overwhelmed.

📧 Email & Phishing Protection

🔍

Verify Sender Domains

Always check the actual sender email address, not just the display name. Hover over email addresses to reveal the real sender. Be wary of domains that don't match the organization name.

❌ Wrong: support@apple.security-update.com
✅ Right: support@apple.com
🔗

Never Click Suspicious Links

Don't click links in emails, even if they look official. Instead, go directly to the website by typing the URL yourself or using a bookmark. Hover over links to see where they actually go.

⚠️

Beware of Urgency

Phishing emails often create artificial urgency: "Your account will be deleted", "Verify immediately", "Urgent action required". Legitimate organizations rarely use such tactics. Take time to think.

📎

Don't Download Unexpected Attachments

Never download attachments from unknown sources. Even if they appear to come from trusted contacts (whose accounts might be compromised), be cautious. Attachments often contain malware.

🔤

Check Spelling & Grammar

Legitimate organizations proofread their emails. Phishing emails often contain typos, grammatical errors, or awkward phrasing. If an email looks poorly written, it's likely phishing.

👤

Look for Personalization

Legitimate emails address you by name and reference specific information about you. Generic greetings like "Dear User" or "Dear Valued Customer" are red flags for phishing.

🔐 Password & Authentication Security

🔑

Create Strong, Unique Passwords

Use passwords with at least 12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across accounts. Different passwords for each account limits damage if one is compromised.

❌ Wrong: password123, john@1995
✅ Right: Tr0pic@l$Sunset#2024!Secure
📱

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds an extra security layer. Even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account without the second factor (phone code, biometric, security key). Enable MFA on all important accounts.

💾

Use a Password Manager

Password managers securely store your passwords and can generate strong ones. They only fill passwords on legitimate sites, not phishing sites. This significantly reduces phishing success. Examples: Bitwarden, 1Password, LastPass.

🚫

Never Share Your Password

No legitimate organization will ask for your password via email, phone, or web form. IT support won't ask for your password. Real companies have other ways to verify your identity. Anyone asking for a password is suspicious.

🔄

Change Passwords Regularly

Change passwords for important accounts every 90 days. If you suspect a breach, change immediately. Monitor accounts for suspicious activity and change passwords of any compromised accounts.

🔑

Use Security Keys When Possible

Hardware security keys (like YubiKey) are the most secure form of 2FA. They work only with the legitimate website, so phishing sites can't use them. They're immune to most phishing attacks.

🌐 Browsing & Website Security

🔒

Look for HTTPS & Padlock

Always verify the padlock icon (🔒) in the address bar. The URL should start with HTTPS (not HTTP). This means the connection is encrypted. However, phishers can also use HTTPS, so always verify the domain name too!

📍

Check the Full URL

Read the complete URL in the address bar, not just what's visible. Phishers use similar-looking URLs like "amaz0n.com" or "paypa1.com". Click on the address bar to see the full URL.

🔖

Bookmark Important Sites

Bookmark the official websites you use regularly (bank, email, social media). Access them through bookmarks instead of clicking links. This ensures you always go to the legitimate site.

🛡️

Keep Browser Updated

Update your browser regularly to patch security vulnerabilities. Many phishing attacks exploit outdated browsers. Enable automatic updates for your browser, operating system, and software.

🧩

Use Security Extensions

Browser extensions can help identify phishing sites. Try uBlock Origin, Bitwarden, or browser-built security features. These can block malicious sites and warn you about phishing attempts.

⚙️

Be Careful with Auto-Fill

Be careful with browser auto-fill features. While convenient, they can sometimes fill credentials on phishing sites. Verify the URL before allowing auto-fill, and use password managers instead for sensitive information.

👥 Social Engineering & General Security

📞

Verify Requests Through Different Channels

If someone claims to be from your organization asking for information, verify through a different channel. If they email, call them. If they call, email them. This confirms they are who they claim to be.

😌

Trust Your Instincts

If something feels wrong, it probably is. You don't need to respond immediately. Take time to verify. It's better to miss an urgent email than to fall for phishing. Your gut feeling is often right.

👁️

Be Cautious in Public WiFi

Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on public WiFi. Attackers can intercept data on public networks. If you must, use a VPN to encrypt your connection. Never access banking or email on unsecured WiFi.

🚨

Report Phishing & Suspicious Activity

Report phishing emails to your IT security team and the organization being impersonated. Report phishing to anti-phishing organizations. This helps protect others and improves defenses against similar attacks.

📚

Stay Educated

Keep learning about cybersecurity threats and best practices. Threats evolve constantly. Take security training courses, read security blogs, and stay updated. Knowledge is your best defense.

📋

Understand Your Rights

Know that you have rights regarding your data. Be cautious about what personal information you share. Only provide necessary information. Don't overshare on social media as attackers use this information for targeted attacks.

✓ Quick Reference Checklist

🚫 Red Flags

  • ☐ Generic greeting ("Dear User")
  • ☐ Urgent language or time pressure
  • ☐ Suspicious URL or domain
  • ☐ Request for passwords/credentials
  • ☐ Grammar and spelling errors
  • ☐ Links from unknown sources
  • ☐ Unexpected attachments
  • ☐ Too good to be true offers

✅ Green Flags

  • ☐ Personalized greeting (your name)
  • ☐ Official domain name verified
  • ☐ HTTPS with padlock
  • ☐ Professional layout
  • ☐ Proper spelling and grammar
  • ☐ Relevant to your situation
  • ☐ Contact information provided
  • ☐ Legitimate request or offer
